Competition Overview

To further promote the practical utility and controllable generation applicability of Disentangled Representation Learning (DRL), we organize a competition as part of the workshop. The primary goal of this competition is to promote the development and evaluation of DRL methods on realistic datasets, thereby accelerating the transition of disentangled models from theoretical research to practical applications.

Our competition is divided into two main categories:

1. Single-Factor Challenge

This track provides a large number of images with corresponding text descriptions. Participants will be evaluated using disentanglement metrics such as DCI (Disentanglement, Completeness, and Informativeness) to assess the quality of learned representations.

2. Multi-Factor Challenge

This track is further divided into two sub-tracks:

  • General Image Dataset Track: Focusing on everyday scenarios with multiple disentanglement factors.
  • Autonomous Driving Dataset Track: Specifically designed for autonomous driving scenarios with various environmental factors.

Each sub-track has two leaderboards:

  • Overall Leaderboard: All methods are evaluated and ranked here.
  • Disentanglement Leaderboard: Only disentanglement models are evaluated and ranked here.

Disentanglement models will be evaluated on both leaderboards, while other methods will only be evaluated on the overall leaderboard.

The top three winners from each leaderboard will be invited to attend the ICCV conference in person to share their approaches and insights.

By training and evaluating models on controllable generation data, our aim is to foster meaningful advancements in the DRL community and facilitate its integration into practical scenarios.

Topic 1: Disentangled Representation Learning for Controllable Generation

Develop disentangled representation learning methods for controllable generation tasks, such as image generation, text-to-image generation, and video generation. The goal is to enable fine-grained control over the generation process through disentangled representations.

  • Develop DRL methods for controllable generation
  • Demonstrate fine-grained control over generation attributes
  • Evaluate the quality and diversity of generated content

Topic 2: Disentangled Representation Learning for Autonomous Driving

Apply disentangled representation learning to autonomous driving tasks, such as scene understanding, trajectory prediction, and decision making. The goal is to improve the robustness, interpretability, and generalization capabilities of autonomous driving systems through disentangled representations.

  • Apply DRL to autonomous driving tasks
  • Improve robustness and generalization in diverse environments
  • Enhance interpretability of driving decisions

Paper Submission

The tentative program committee (PC) consists of experts in the list of organizers. This committee will be responsible for evaluating the submitted papers, recommending accepted papers for the workshop, and ensuring the quality of submissions aligns with the scope and goals of the workshop.

All accepted papers will be published in the ICCV 2025 workshop proceedings, which will be officially distributed through the conference's channels and made accessible to all ICCV attendees.

  • Winners of each topic will receive prize money and certificates, with specific prize amounts to be announced at the start of the challenge.
  • Winning teams will be invited to present their methods at the ICCV 2025 DRL for Real Workshop and will have the opportunity to publish their work in a special issue.
  • All participants will receive participation certificates, recognizing their contributions to disentangled representation learning research.
